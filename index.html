Miday at "Rolling Thunder", Conway, Arkansas

Dave Ward mansion in Conway, Arkansas has many stories of past and present.

Dec 07, 2025

Conway, Arkansas - For years, a large, unfinished home sat quietly along Dave Ward Drive, earning the nickname "Dave Ward Mansion" among locals. Once envisioned as a grand private residence, the house never reached completion, leaving behind a story more about ambition got awry than scandal. Construction began in the 1990's, with plans for an expansive home featuring multiple levels and wide, open rooms. The structure promised to be one of the most impressive in Conway, but the project soon ran into trouble. Costs rose quickly, upkeeping became a challenge, and the sheer scale of the building proved overwhelming for the owners. By the early 2000's, the mansion had been abandoned. Partially finished rooms, exposed staircases, and incomplete spaces turned the house into a ghostly momunment to what could have been. With no residents or utilities, it fell into decay and became a magnet for curious teens, photographers, and local explorers. The mansion met its dramatic end in 2013 when a fire consumed the already derelict building. Authorities reported that most of the structure was destroyed, leaving only the foundation and scattered remains. No evidence of foul play has been publicly reported, and the damage was too extensive to seek any restoration. since then, the site has remained empty, a quiet reminder of a dream left unfinished. Even tho the mansion no longer stands, it continues to capture the imagination of the community. Over time, it has become a fixture of Conway folklore, a story that blurs the line between myth and memory. Today, the lot slong Dave Ward Drive remains still and vacant, a silent testament to ambition that never fully materialized.
